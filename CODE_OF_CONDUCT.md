# Code of Conduct

Altitude is an independent project with a small, focused community. This code of conduct is short because the expectations are simple: be a person worth collaborating with.

---

## The standard

Participate the way you would at a job you respect.

That means:

- Respectful, direct communication — disagree with approaches, not people
- Constructive feedback focused on the work, with specific technical reasoning
- Accepting that maintainer decisions are final
- Giving credit when your contribution builds on someone else's idea
- Assuming good faith unless you have specific reason not to

That means not:

- Personal attacks, harassment, or intimidation of any kind
- Discriminatory language or behaviour — based on gender, sexuality, race, religion, nationality, disability, or anything else
- Publishing private information about anyone without their consent
- Conduct you wouldn't want attributed to you professionally
- Belittling contributions because of who made them rather than the work itself

The bar for contributions to Altitude is high. The bar for how we treat each other is the same.

---

## Scope

This applies to all Altitude community spaces: GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, code reviews, and any other space where someone is representing or interacting with this project.

---

## Enforcement

The project maintainer — **waleedahmedja** — has final authority on enforcement decisions.

Violations may result in:

1. A private conversation about the behaviour and why it was problematic
2. A formal warning with specific expectations going forward
3. Temporary or permanent removal from community spaces

Reports can be made by opening a private GitHub Issue or contacting the maintainer directly. All reports are handled with discretion. Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is itself a violation.

---

## Why this exists

Not because problems are expected — but because a written standard makes the implicit explicit. Everyone knows what kind of space this is before they arrive.

It's a space for people who care about making good software.
