# Code of Conduct

**Altitude — Live Flight Tracker**

---

## The Short Version

Be a person worth collaborating with.

Treat contributors, users, and maintainers the way you'd want to be treated if you'd just spent your weekend building something and were brave enough to share it publicly.

That's genuinely most of it. The rest is just the formal language that makes it enforceable.

---

## Our Standards

This community works when people show up with curiosity, honesty, and patience. Specifically, we expect:

- **Respect for different experience levels.** Someone asking a basic question deserves a real answer, not condescension. Everyone started somewhere.
- **Constructive disagreement.** You can think an approach is wrong and say so. Be specific about the technical reason. Don't make it personal.
- **Credit where it's due.** If someone's idea informed your contribution, say so.
- **Good faith.** Assume questions and criticism come from a place of genuine interest unless you have a specific reason to believe otherwise.
- **Patience with maintainers.** This project is maintained by independent developers in their own time. Reviews take time. Silence isn't rejection.

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following will not be tolerated in any project space — issues, PRs, discussions, or anywhere the Altitude community interacts:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind
- Discriminatory language or behaviour based on gender, sexuality, race, religion, nationality, disability, or any other personal characteristic
- Publishing someone's private information without consent
- Sustained disruption of discussions or deliberate derailing of conversations
- Dismissing or belittling contributions because of who made them rather than the work itself

This list is illustrative, not exhaustive. The standard is: would a reasonable person feel unwelcome or unsafe? If yes, it doesn't belong here.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- The GitHub repository — issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews, commit messages
- Any official communication channels associated with Altitude
- Public representation of the project (e.g. if someone presents Altitude at an event)

It applies to everyone who participates — contributors, maintainers, and users.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct:

**Email:** conduct@altitudeapp.dev *(placeholder — update before launch)*

Reports are handled confidentially. You will receive an acknowledgement within 72 hours and a resolution update within 7 days.

If the report involves a maintainer, it will be handled by another maintainer or a trusted third party.

We take every report seriously. Retaliation against anyone who makes a good-faith report is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct.

---

## Enforcement

Depending on the nature and severity of the violation, responses may include:

**1. Correction** — A private conversation about the behaviour and why it was problematic.

**2. Warning** — A formal warning with specific expectations for future conduct.

**3. Temporary Ban** — Temporary removal from project spaces with conditions for return.

**4. Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal from all project spaces for severe, sustained, or repeated violations.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.
